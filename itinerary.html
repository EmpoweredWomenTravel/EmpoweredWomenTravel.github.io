The Heartbeat of Cuba is its Capital — Havana

Old Havana (Habana Vieja) is the city-center (downtown) and one of the 15 municipalities (or boroughs) which form Havana, Cuba. It has the second-highest population density in the city and contains the core of the original city of Havana. Old Havana is the largest Colonial center in Latin America. Stroll down the walkable streets and admire the fascinating architecture, historic buildings, churches, galleries, museums, and plazas.

Vedado

Vedado is a more modern part of the city than the areas to the east, developed in the first half of the 20th century, during the “Republic” period. It can be described as the city's “most affluent” section. Exploring this neighborhood you'll notice that Vedado is a stark contrast to Old Havana because it has the feel of strolling down Coral Gables in Florida.

Miramar

Miramar is the home of many embassies, many of them located on Quinta Avenida (Fifth Avenue). Once we tour this neighborhood you'll understand why. Prior to the Revolution, the neighborhood was home to many of Havana's upscale residents. There are many large houses and mansions here. This district and the Country Club (Cubanacán) were the most glamorous spaces in the Havana of the 50s.

Cuba's Quiet Jewel — Vinales — The Soul of Cuba

Vinales and its surrounding valley was Fidel Castro's favorite part of Cuba. It is an extremely picturesque town, and its valley of the same name is the heart and soul of Cuba's tobacco farming culture. The exact opposite of Havana, this lush sleepy town offers opportunities to relax and fall in love with the quintessential rural Cuban lifestyle. Such is the cultural value of Vinales that UNESCO designated the valley a “heritage” site in 1999. With its trademark karst (eroded limestone) pillars called ‘mogotes’ rising up like sentinels from the valley floor, it really doesn't get much more scenic than this. The perfect place to relax, enjoy the natural wonders and take great pictures.

Varadero

Varadero is a resort town in the province of Matanzas, Cuba and one of the largest resort areas in the Caribbean. Varadero is a tropical paradise known for the sparkly blue waters and sandy white beaches. Varadero Beach came in at number two in 2019 for the World's Top Beaches. Varadero, also referred to as Playa Azul (Blue Beach), is a good destination for relaxing, entertainment, fishing or taking excursions. There are some really interesting women-owned businesses in this town that we will visit and support, along with great women-owned restaurants.
